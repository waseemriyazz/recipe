Paced Repetition Method

    Ingredients:
  • Study materials (textbooks, notes, flashcards)
  • Timer or stopwatch
  • Quiet study environment
    Instructions:
  1. Begin by organizing your study materials into smaller chunks or flashcards.
  2. Set a timer for a predetermined amount of time (e.g., 20 minutes).
  3. Start studying a set of flashcards or a particular section of the material.
  4. Once the timer goes off, take a short break and put the studied material aside.
  5. After a brief break, review the same set of flashcards or material again.
  6. Repeat this process of studying and reviewing the material at gradually increasing intervals.
  7. For example, review the material again after a day, then after a few days, a week, and so on.
  8. Focus more on the flashcards or sections that are more challenging to reinforce learning.
  9. Continue reviewing the material periodically, spacing out the intervals based on your understanding and retention.
  10. The spaced repetition method helps in strengthening long-term memory and retaining information effectively over time.
